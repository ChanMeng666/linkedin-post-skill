🚀 The AI-Native Developer Mindset: Why Hand-Coding Everything Is the New Horse-and-Buggy 🐎🚗

A student recently asked me how to ace technical interviews when their portfolio was built with AI assistance. The answer surprises most devs — so I'm sharing it.

Hiding your AI workflow doesn't make you look stronger. It makes you look outdated.

Here's the 4-part framework I gave them 👇

🧠 1/ Master Prompt Engineering as a Core Skill
Stop winging it. Study the official docs.
→ Be explicit, structured, use XML tags
→ Treat prompts like API contracts, not casual chat
→ Claude's prompting guide is required reading

🏗️ 2/ Rethink Architecture: One Monolith, One Codebase
The microservices dogma breaks under AI-assisted dev.
→ Single codebase = AI sees the full E2E logic in ONE context window
→ Multi-repo = fragmented context = worse output
→ You own the architecture. AI handles the typing.

💼 3/ Ace Interviews by Owning Your AI Workflow
Every modern company uses AI to ship faster. Pretending you don't will count against you.
✅ Explain the WHY (architecture & tech stack choices)
✅ Show product vision (market, business logic, tradeoffs)
✅ Demonstrate AI orchestration as a senior skill
❌ Don't stress about reciting line-by-line syntax

🎯 4/ Real Projects Beat Toy Projects — Always
Production-grade apps with real users > theoretical demos.

The mental shift:
🐎 Old world → memorize syntax, hand-type everything
🚗 New world → architect systems, orchestrate AI, ship products

Stop being a code typist. Start being a system architect. ✨
